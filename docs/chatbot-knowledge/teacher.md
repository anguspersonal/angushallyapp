**Where I taught:** Burnt Mill Academy, Harlow, Essex — TeachFirst leadership-development placement 2016–2018. GCSE foundation and higher tier, plus A-Level mathematics. PGCE completed alongside the placement.

**Tagline:** *"Two years teaching GCSE and A-Level maths in Harlow. The hardest thing I've done. Where I learned to operate."*

**What teaching taught me** (the transferable-skills narrative the page leads with):

- Knowing the gap between your model of a topic and the student's — the same skill that lets me brief engineers as a non-engineer and brief investors as a non-investor.
- Composure under low-status conditions (Year 10 bottom set on a Friday afternoon).
- Routine as a force multiplier — lesson structure underwrites any operating system.
- Marking 31 books at midnight on Sunday — operator stamina starts here.

**Pedagogical principles** (the framing for edtech / tutoring audiences):

1. Diagnostic-first — find the misconception, then teach the correction.
2. Worked examples beat explanation; modelling out loud beats both.
3. Spaced retrieval over re-exposure.
4. Application in a non-routine context is the real test of mastery.
5. Willingness to be wrong in front of you is the biggest determinant of A-Level outcomes.
