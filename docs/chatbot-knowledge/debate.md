**Tagline:** *"Competed at the World University Debating Championships. Founded a school's first debate program."*

**The two arcs:**

1. **Competitor — LSE Debate Team, 2013–2016.** Member then **General Secretary**. Three full seasons of inter-university competitions across the UK and continental Europe (British Parliamentary format). **WUDC 2015 in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia — finished 232nd out of 731 individual debaters globally** on the WUDC 2015 tab. Hundreds of competitions total.

2. **Coach — Burnt Mill Academy, Harlow, 2016–2018.** During the TeachFirst placement (see `/teacher`), founded the school's debate program from scratch — no prior infrastructure — and took Burnt Mill to **its first inter-school debate competitions**.

The combination is rare: most school debate coaches have either competed but never built a program, or built a program but never reached the top tables.

**What debate taught (and what coaching debate teaches students):**

- **Argue both sides at full strength before you commit** — the single most under-priced cognitive skill in business.
- **Hold structured argument under time pressure** — 7 minutes to make the case, 3 minutes of points-of-information, immediate adjudication.
- **Performance discipline under hostile examination** — what survives PoIs survives investor Q&A and board challenges.
- **Adversarial respect** — disagreement is the point; treating opponents as enemies is the amateur move.

**Coaching principles** (the page leads with these for the schools/edtech audience): format first; PoIs are the test; roleplay the judge in feedback; match the motion to the student early; travel the team.

**Continuing relevance:** HeyLina investor Q&A, briefing clinical advisors, engineering trade-off debates — the cross-examination muscle stays live. Also surfaces in the `/spec-debate` Claude Code skill, a five-agent debate orchestration scoring a spec against a rubric.
